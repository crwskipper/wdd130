History

River rafting has a rich history that spans millennia, beginning with early civilizations using rudimentary rafts for transportation and trade along rivers like the Nile. As exploration expanded, adventurers embarked on daring expeditions, navigating treacherous waters in search of new lands and resources. In the mid-20th century, river rafting evolved into a recreational activity, gaining popularity as people sought thrilling outdoor adventures.

With advancements in equipment and safety measures, rafting became more accessible, leading to its global expansion as enthusiasts explored rivers on every continent. Today, river rafting encompasses a spectrum of experiences, from adrenaline-pumping whitewater rapids to leisurely floats through scenic landscapes, reflecting humanity's enduring connection to the power and beauty of rivers.
